Plot 2 – Sleep vs Productivity

Author: Sujan Pokhrel

Hypothesis

As sleep hours increase, productivity improves. However, higher stress or extended screen time may lower this positive effect. I expect high productivity at around 7–8 hours of sleep with low stress and balanced screen time.

Question

How does sleep duration affect productivity when considering stress and screen time?

Data Elements

  • X-axis: Sleep Hours (5.5–9 hours)
  • Y-axis: Productivity (1–5 scale)
  • Circle Size: Screen Time (hours)
  • Circle Color: Stress Level (1–5, blue = low, red = high)

How Array.filter() Was Used

The Array.filter() method removes days with less than 6 hours of sleep, helping focus on healthy sleep patterns and clearer data relationships. This made the visualization more meaningful and accurate.

Reflection

The scatterplot shows that individuals with higher sleep duration tend to have better productivity. Redder circles (high stress) cluster around lower productivity, showing how stress limits performance. Filtering the dataset helped highlight consistent sleep trends and removed unnecessary noise.
