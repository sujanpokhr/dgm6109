Hypothesis

Higher daily screen time is associated with increased stress levels.

About This Visualization

This visualization uses a radial (polar) layout to display daily wellness data collected over a 30-day period. Each day is represented by a single “petal” positioned around a circle. The design allows multiple aspects of wellness to be shown at the same time, making patterns and relationships easier to observe than in a traditional linear chart.

The visualization was specifically designed to explore how screen time relates to stress, while also considering the roles of sleep and productivity.

How to Read the Visualization

Each visual encoding represents a different variable from the dataset:

  • Angle (around the circle) represents daily screen time in hours (4–10 hours).
  • Distance from the center represents sleep duration (fewer hours closer to center).
  • Color represents stress level (blue = low stress, red = high stress).
  • Size represents productivity level (larger = more productive).

Background rings and radial guide lines act as axis equivalents, allowing values to be read without interaction.

Data Description

The dataset contains 30 consecutive days of manually collected personal data, spanning from October 8 to November 6, 2025.

Objective measures include total screen time, time spent on social media, time spent on school activities, and hours of sleep. Subjective measures include self-rated stress and productivity on a scale of 1 to 5, recorded each evening.

Observed Patterns and Trends

Several clear patterns emerge from the visualization. Days with higher screen time tend to cluster in regions with warmer colors, indicating higher stress levels. These days are often closer to the center of the chart, showing reduced sleep duration.

In contrast, days with lower screen time are more frequently associated with cooler colors, greater distance from the center, and larger petal sizes, indicating lower stress, more sleep, and higher productivity.

One especially notable pattern is a sharp shift in wellness outcomes once daily screen time exceeds approximately eight hours. Above this threshold, stress increases rapidly while sleep and productivity decrease.

Conclusion

The visualization supports the hypothesis that higher screen time is associated with increased stress. However, it also reveals that sleep plays a critical mediating role in this relationship. High screen time often coincides with reduced sleep, which in turn contributes to higher stress and lower productivity.

Rather than a gradual decline, the data suggests a threshold effect: wellness outcomes worsen significantly once screen time exceeds a certain point.
